Christ the Redeemer (Brazil)

High above Rio de Janeiro, Christ the Redeemer stands as a symbol of faith, peace, and unity. This colossal statue of Jesus Christ, with arms outstretched in welcome, overlooks the vibrant city from the summit of Mount Corcovado.

The idea for the statue emerged in the 1850s, but it wasn’t until the 1920s that it gained momentum. Designed by Brazilian engineer Heitor da Silva Costa and French sculptor Paul Landowski, the statue was completed in 1931. Standing 98 feet tall, with its arms spanning 92 feet, it is made of reinforced concrete and covered in soapstone, chosen for its durability and smooth finish.

Christ the Redeemer is more than a religious monument—it’s a cultural icon. The site attracts millions of visitors annually, who ascend Mount Corcovado by train or trail to enjoy breathtaking views of Rio’s beaches, forests, and urban sprawl. At night, the statue is illuminated, creating a striking image against the dark sky.

Declared one of the New Seven Wonders of the World, Christ the Redeemer represents hope and hospitality, embodying the welcoming spirit of Brazil.
