Taj Mahal (India)

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is a symbol of love and one of the most exquisite examples of Mughal architecture. Built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, this white marble mausoleum was completed in 1648 after more than 20 years of construction.

The Taj Mahalâ€™s design features intricate inlays of semi-precious stones, calligraphy, and floral motifs. Its symmetry is complemented by expansive gardens and a reflecting pool, which enhances its beauty.

Beyond its architectural splendor, the Taj Mahal tells a poignant story of devotion. Declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site, it attracts millions of visitors annually, inspiring awe with its timeless elegance.
