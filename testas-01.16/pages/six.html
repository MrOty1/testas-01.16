The Colosseum

The Colosseum, or Flavian Amphitheater, is a monumental testament to the grandeur of ancient Rome. Completed in 80 AD under Emperor Titus, this massive amphitheater could hold up to 50,000 spectators, who gathered to watch gladiator battles, animal hunts, and public spectacles.

Built using concrete and stone, the Colosseum featured advanced engineering, including a retractable awning system to shield spectators from the sun and a complex underground network for staging events.
